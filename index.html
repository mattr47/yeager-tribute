Chuck Yeager

Record Setting Test Pilot

Brief Timeline from Yeager's Life

  • February 13, 1923 - Born in West Virginia
  • June, 1941 - Graduated High School
  • September, 1941 - Joined US Army Air Forces
  • October 12, 1944 - Became an "ace in a day," downing five enemy aircraft in a single mission
  • February 26, 1945 - Married Glennis Dickhouse
  • October 14, 1947 - Broke the sound barrier, at Mach 1.07 at an altitude of 45,000 ft
  • June 22, 1969 - Promoted to Brigadier General
  • March 1, 1975 - Retired from military service

If you can walk away from a landing, it's a good landing. If you use the airplane the next day, it's an outstanding landing. Chuck Yeager

Chuck Yeager contributed much to the current state of aviation and space flight we have today. If you would like to learn more about him please read his Wikipedia entry.
